Debriefing Information


This research employed a randomised experimental design. This means that there were 4 alternative versions of the survey displayed to different participants. The major difference was the content of the article shown at the beginning of the survey. There was also a control condition in which no article was shown.


Two newspaper style articles were composed for the purpose of this study by the study authors. The news articles did not appear in this exact form in any newspaper. Despite being composed for the study the researchers ensured that all information reported in these articles was an accurate plain language account of current research.


A media release by a fictitious community organisation was also composed by the study authors for the purpose of the study. Although this organisation does not exist, the release was closely modelled on historical documents released by comparable community organisations in Australia.


If you have questions about this feature of the study you are free to contact the study author using the contact information below.

Seeking Help For Gambling-Related Harm


This study has included a number of questions about gambling-related issues. If you are experiencing any gambling-related distress or if you have similar concerns about a friend or relative you are encouraged to seek support and advice.


Listed below are the contact details for Gambler's Help, a counselling and support service funded by the Victorian Responsible Gambling Foundation. These services include:


  • Mental health counselling
  • Specialised financial counselling to help individuals recover from debt
  • Peer support from volunteers who have had personal experience with gambling harm
  • Community education about the early signs of gambling harm
  • Support and information for family and friends

Anyone who has been impacted by gambling-related difficulties can call this helpline. Operators can provide the contact details for your nearest Gambler's Help service or, if you prefer, a service away from where you live. Calls to the Helpline are free from landlines, and won't be listed on your landline phone bill (although may be listed on mobile bills).
