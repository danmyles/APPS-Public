What is this study about?

This research study aims to survey community opinions regarding a range of political and social issues related to pokies gambling. Pokies gambling has been a topic of political debate and has drawn attention in a number of news media outlets. We are interested in how the community views and responds to these discussions.


Why were you chosen for this research?

You have been invited to participate in this research because you have previously indicated that you are willing to be contacted to participate in survey research.


Why were you chosen for this research?

Anyone living in Victoria or NSW who is over the age of 18 is eligible to participate in this research. We want to hear from a wide range of views on this important social issue. We would like to hear from you whether or not you have had any personal experience with gambling.


What are you being asked to do?

You will first be asked to provide some basic demographic information. We collect this information to ensure that our survey has reached a wide range of Australians living in NSW and Victoria. You will then be asked to read about and consider a number of topics related to pokies gambling. Most questions will ask you to respond to statements using a standard response scale. Towards the end of the study you will be asked to view a short video (2 min 34 seconds) depicting a poker machine in operation.


The survey should take not take longer than 25 minutes to complete.


Participation is entirely voluntary

Participation in this research study is voluntary and you are completely free to withdraw at any time. Any associations you have with Monash University or the researcher will not be affected by not participating or withdrawing from the study prior to conclusion.


You may withdraw by closing the survey window or the web browser in which the survey is open. Note that any responses submitted prior to closing the survey window will already have been recorded.


If you wish to withdraw your responses, you will need to contact the researcher directly. Please note that it may not be possible to remove or delete your responses following consent to participate in this study. This is a consequence of the study being anonymised to protect your identity. Also note that in contacting the researcher you may reveal your identity through your email address or correspondence.


What are the potential risks and benefits of taking part?

This study explores issues related to gambling. We acknowledge the that topic of gambling may be upsetting or confronting for some individuals. Contact details for a number of gambling and psychological counselling services are provided at the conclusion of this study as well as at the bottom of this page.


There are no other obvious or known risks associated with participating in the study. Should you choose to participate, you will be contributing to a research effort that aims to improve our understanding of this important social issue.


Confidentiality

All data collected in the study will be anonymised. In order to protect your identity, your responses will be assigned an anonymous I.D. number. It is highly unlikely that it would be possible to identify you personally from your survey responses.


Online survey platforms collect device information to help identify duplicate or automated responding. This information will not be used to identify you personally and will be destroyed after it has been used to ensure data quality.


How will my information be used and stored?

Data will be collated, statistically analysed and may form part of a research thesis. The results may also be presented at research conferences, presentations or included in a published research journal. Public presentations and publications will only report aggregate and anonymised data and will not identify you personally.


All data collected will be stored in accordance with Monash University regulations. Data collected for this project may be retained indefinitely or for an extended period. Data collected for the current study may be used in future research projects or made available to other researchers in accordance with open science principles.


Anonymous data may be made available to other researchers at public research institutions or through the Open Science Framework. This is to allow for the possibility of peer review, replication studies, re-analyses, quality control and meta-analyses. If made available to other researchers, these data will be anonymous and made available under a Creative Commons Non-Commercial Licence that forbids the use of these data for commercial gain.
